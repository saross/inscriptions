## 2. Friday 2026-04-24 draft focus — minimum thresholds

Adela's stated priority: first-pass minimum-threshold analysis for
subsets of inscriptions (city-level, decade-level). SPA runs,
population mapping, and interpretation can wait.

**Must contain:**

- Clear statement of the question: *how many inscriptions / letters
  do we need at each subset level for a meaningful SPA?*
- Chosen method (Decision 2): frequentist permutation-envelope
  simulation via tempun + scipy port, ~200 LOC Python.
- Effect-size bracket (Decision 5): (a) 50% sustained deviation
  over ≥50-year window as headline; (b) doubling/halving over
  ≥25-year window; (c) 20% deviation over ≥25-year window.
- Distribution (Decision 4): uniform primary; trapezoidal
  mentioned as Week-2 sensitivity run.
- First-pass power curve at the city and decade level, showing
  threshold *n* at which the significance test reaches power ≥ 0.8
  at α = 0.05 for each of the three effect sizes.
- Caveats: what this first pass assumes (effect-size choices, null
  model, distributional assumption) and what would refine it.
- *Data artefacts* note (the EDH/EDCS inherited issues —
  working-notes Obs 7) so Adela can factor them into her read.

**Does NOT need to contain** (per Adela): actual SPAs, population
regressions, interpretation of results, full paper-skeleton,
letter-count analysis, or subset-by-subset results.
